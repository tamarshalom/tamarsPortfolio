For my final podcast project, I created an episode of our group podcast series, **Behind the Grades**, which focuses on college students’ mental health. My individual episode is titled *The Pressure of Being Perfect: The Impact of Social Media Comparison on Mental Health*. In this episode, listeners can expect an educational and reflective podcast that explores how social media affects the mental health of college students, especially through comparison, pressure, perfectionism, and self-esteem.

## What this episode covers

This podcast is designed for **college students and other young adults** who may relate to the pressure of constantly comparing themselves to others online. Throughout the episode, I discuss how curated images of success, beauty, productivity, and happiness on social media can make students feel like they are falling behind, even when that is not the reality. I also explain some of the emotional effects of this comparison, including anxiety, stress, self-doubt, and the fear of not being “good enough.” In addition, I talk about **healthy coping strategies**, such as setting boundaries with social media, becoming more aware of harmful content, and reaching out for support when needed.

## Format and nonprofit spotlight

The format of my podcast is an **individual educational podcast** with a reflective tone. This episode does not include a guest interview, but it does include a short **PSA** promoting our chosen nonprofit organization, [**Active Minds**](https://activeminds.org/). Active Minds is a nonprofit dedicated to changing the conversation around mental health and supporting students and young adults through awareness, education, and open dialogue. Their mission connects directly to our group’s overall project theme, which is why they were an especially meaningful choice for this assignment.

## Course context and goals

This project was created for **Strategic Presentation** with **Professor Dorrielan**. My goal with this episode was to create a podcast that is **informative, relatable, and meaningful** for students who may be silently struggling with pressure and comparison. Mental health challenges are often hidden behind academic performance and appearance, and I wanted this episode to help make those struggles more visible and more openly discussed.
