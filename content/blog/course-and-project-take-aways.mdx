Throughout this course, one of the most memorable things I learned is how important the audience is in any strategic presentation. Before this course, I knew that presentations needed to be clear, maybe professional/formal, and organized, but I did not fully understand how much the audience shapes every part of the message. The way a presentation is written and delivered should depend on who is the one listening to it. A presentation for students will look very different from a presentation for a nonprofit and or a business. I learned that if the content does not connect to the audience’s interests or values then even a really well made presentation can lose its impact.

Secondly, another important takeaway from this course is that the presentation needs to be both memorable and relatable. It should not just simply give away information; it should leave the audience with something they can remember after it's done. This could be maybe starting with a strong story, a meaningful visual message, personal connection, and even like the PSA we did in class. Through the podcast project, I learned that relatable content often grabs people's attention more effectively because it makes the topic feel real to the listeners. When an audience can see themselves  in the message or story then they can start to understand why it may matter to them. This means they are way more likely to stay engaged and care about what is being presented overall.

Lastly, a third memorable takeaway from this course was learning what it takes to create a website portfolio. As a CS student I decided on taking the challenge and building my own website from scratch. Meaning “code” it. I wanted the portfolio to really resemble me. Throughout the process it made me think about how I want to present myself, my work, and my experiences to those who view it. It also helped me learn more about myself in ways I never have before since I had to explain things that I had not really ever put into words before. It helped me think over my strengths, projects, and even weaknesses. This overall helped me later on in the semester when it came time for all my interviews. I was smashed with so many interviews on the hunt for a summer internship I was so overwhelmed however, with the help of creating this portfolio I was already one step ahead since I had my ideas in my mind.

 Overall, this course taught me that strategic presentation is not only about speaking or designing slides. It is about understanding your audience and presenting yourself and your ideas in a way that people will remember. I just wanted to end off by saying thank you so much for teaching us this course and allowing for students to bridge their creativity within the assignments. I really enjoyed this class overall and appreciate the time you put into us students.
