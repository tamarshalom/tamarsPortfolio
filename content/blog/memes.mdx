My website topic is the life of a college student whose main interests are building products with AI and  studying computer science. Because my target audience may include classmates, recruiters, people interested in tech, friends, and even professors, I chose memes that connect to my coding culture and experience of working with modern technology. Memes are useful for this topic because they make technical ideas feel more relatable. I also enjoy posting all over different social medias and have noticed that relatable content is what people like to see the most.

Patric Favison explains that an internet meme is “a piece of culture, typically a joke, which gains influence through online transmission.” He also breaks memes into three different parts. He says memes are “ the ideal, the behavior, and the manifestation.” The ideal is the main concept behind the meme, the behavior is how people use it, and the manifestation is the actual version such as the gif or image of the actual meme.

The first meme I chose is Drakeposting. According to Know Your Meme, Drakeposting comes from reaction images taken from Drake’s 2015 “Hotline Bling” music video and is usually used to reject one option while approving another. For my portfolio, this meme relates to the choices developers make when building products: rejecting boring or generic tech ideas and choosing more creative and or efficient AI powered ideas. The ideal is the comparison and preference part. The behavior is users copying the two-panel format and replacing the rejected and approved ideas with their own labels. Lastly, the manifestation I chose is the classic Yellow Drake image, which is different from others because it's simple and easy to recognize.

The second meme is This Is Fine. Know Your Meme explains that it comes from K.C. Green’s Gunshow comic shows a dog calmly sitting in a burning room which usually represents denial or acceptance in such a hopeless situation. This meme has a strong connection to being a computer science student. This is because coding and building products always involve bugs, broken features and the worst, unexpected errors. The ideal is pretending everything is okay during all the chaos. Secondly, the behavior is using the image as a reaction to stressful situations. Lastly, the manifestation I chose is the image that shows the dog sitting in the fire super calmly. This works well for me because the contrast between the calm character and the disaster around it makes the meme much more relatable as I am sure many other college students feel this way.

Lastly, the third meme is the distracted boyfriend. Know Your Meme describes it as an object labeling stock photo series where a man looks back at another woman while his partner looks upset and he's holding his partner's hand. For my portfolio, this meme can represent how easy it is for recruiters and other companies to get distracted by a new candidate because of all of the modern new technologies and frameworks being shipped out. The ideal is this temptation for something when what you already have is good. Secondly, the behavior is labeling each person in the image to represent a conflict between both choices. Lastly, the manifestation I chose is the OG stock photo format, which is super effective because facial expressions already can communicate the jokes before any labels are even added.

Overall, these memes appeal to my target audience because they connect internet humor to real college life experiences in tech. Recruiters and classmates can understand pressures of debugging, choosing between tools,and trying to build something innovative all while getting a good laugh in. Davison also explains that digital memes are both easy to copy and easy to change which helps explain how and why memes spread so quickly online. In my portfolio, these memes can make my interest in product building feel much more creative and approachable to any student classmate or recruiter who comes my way.
